DEAR SELF IN FIVE YEARS AS A CODER

The tech industry is rapidly evolving with time. New innocations are springing up by the day with millions of people adopting technical related approach for sustainability.

I'm writing you this letter because i want to give you some advice that will help you be a better professional. These are all things you'll learn yourself in the next few years.

but this list contains everything i wish someone has told me when i started my career. They are not ordered by any means and are all equally important.

Run a marathon, not a sprint

The road to becoming a good software engineer is a long one. Dont't rush things, and dont give up just because you are not getting an easy and fast win

Take your time to learn and become proficient in the topics you are interested in. Remember that this is a marathon, not a sprint.

Jump on the white board and not on the keyboard

When you have to design a new feature or a new system, don't jump on the keyboard to start coding. The "muscle" you have to train and use as an engineer is your brain, not your

thoughts.

Oh, when i say "Whiteboard" I mean "anything that can help you think",be it pen and paper, a notebook application, draw..io, and so on

Choose life, not work.

In the IT field, it's easy to focus too much on work. Afterall, for most of us,it is not just a job, it is our passion. Remember that work is important but life is more important.

Live a meaningful and rich life. Play sports, read books, find hobbies, travel and see the beautiful world we are living in. Hang out with friends, find a partner for your life and give

to your partner all the love, attention and support that you can.

You'll be surprisedhow much having a life will improve you as a proffesional.Also add these to your catalogue of skills;

  1. Proficiency with programming languages
  2. Learning conceptsand applying them to other problems
  3. Communication skills
  4. Writing skills
  5. inquisitiveness
  6. Self motivation

    7. That's all i can tell you right now. I still have a lot to learn.

    One last note enjoy the ride!
